Australia is home to many unique and fascinating animals. Kangaroos are marsupials that are native to Australia and are known for their powerful hind legs and ability to hop. Koalas are tree-dwelling marsupials that feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves. The platypus is one of the most unusual mammals in the world, being one of only two egg-laying mammals. Wombats are burrowing marsupials with strong claws and teeth. The emu is Australia's largest bird and cannot fly. Dingoes are wild dogs that are found throughout Australia. The kookaburra is a kingfisher bird known for its distinctive laughing call. Tasmanian devils are carnivorous marsupials found only in Tasmania. The echidna is another egg-laying mammal found in Australia. Australia also has many venomous snakes and spiders, including the inland taipan, which is considered the world's most venomous snake.
