Australia is home to many famous landmarks and natural wonders. The Sydney Opera House is one of the most recognizable buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Sydney Harbour Bridge is an iconic steel arch bridge that spans Sydney Harbour. The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system and is visible from outer space. Uluru, also known as Ayers Rock, is a massive sandstone monolith in the Northern Territory. The Twelve Apostles are limestone stacks off the shore of Victoria. The Blue Mountains are a mountain range in New South Wales known for their blue haze. The Great Ocean Road is a scenic coastal drive in Victoria. The Royal Exhibition Building in Melbourne is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Port Arthur Historic Site in Tasmania is a former penal colony. The Kakadu National Park is a vast protected area in the Northern Territory known for its Aboriginal rock art and diverse wildlife.
