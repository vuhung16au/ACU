Australian cuisine reflects the country's multicultural heritage and unique ingredients. Vegemite is a dark brown food paste made from yeast extract, which is a quintessential Australian spread. Meat pies are a traditional Australian food, often eaten at sporting events. Lamingtons are sponge cakes coated in chocolate and coconut. Pavlova is a meringue-based dessert named after the Russian ballerina Anna Pavlova. Tim Tams are chocolate biscuits that are extremely popular in Australia. Anzac biscuits are sweet biscuits made from rolled oats, flour, sugar, butter, and golden syrup. Barramundi is a popular fish in Australian cuisine. Kangaroo meat is consumed in Australia and is considered a lean, healthy protein. Australian wine is world-renowned, particularly from regions like the Barossa Valley and Hunter Valley. The country is also known for its high-quality beef and lamb. Australian coffee culture is strong, with Melbourne being particularly famous for its coffee scene.
